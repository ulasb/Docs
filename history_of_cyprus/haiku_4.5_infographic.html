A Brief History of Cyprus

From ancient times to modern partition

Early Settlement
8000 BCE onwards
Earliest human settlement from the Levant. Third-largest Mediterranean island with strategic importance along major trade routes.
Ancient & Classical Rule
333 BCE - 58 BCE
Controlled by Phoenicians, Assyrians, and Egyptians. Alexander the Great conquered in 333 BCE, followed by Ptolemaic Dynasty for ~250 years.
Roman & Byzantine Rule
58 BCE - 1200s CE
Nearly 700 years under Rome. Byzantine rule for ~800 years, establishing strong Christian culture that persists today.
Arab-Byzantine Conflict
7th - 10th Centuries
Island jointly administered between Byzantine and Arab forces, with both powers collecting taxes.
Frankish & Venetian Rule
1192 - 1570
Lusignan family ruled 1192-1489, introducing Catholic Christianity. Venetians controlled 1489-1570, creating lasting religious divisions.
Ottoman Period
1570 - 1878
Over 300 years of Ottoman rule. Turkish Muslim population migrated, creating Greek Christian majority and Turkish Muslim minority.
British Colonial Rule
1878 - 1960
Ceded after Russo-Turkish War, formally annexed in 1914. Greek Cypriots sought union with Greece; Turkish Cypriots wanted status quo.
Independence & Crisis
1960 - 1974
Independence August 16, 1960. Intercommunal violence in 1963. UN peacekeeping deployed 1964. 1974 military coup and Turkish invasion caused partition.
Modern Division
1974 - Present
Turkish control of northern 36%. UN buffer zone divides island for 50+ years. Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus declared 1983. Republic of Cyprus EU member since 2004.
