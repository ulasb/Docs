From Ancient Copper Traders to Europe's Last Divided Capital

Ancient Cyprus
12,000 BCE - 333 BCE
Settlement dating back 12,000 years. Named after copper ("kypros"). Mycenaean Greeks established city-kingdoms, shaping the island's Greek identity.
Age of Empires
333 BCE - 1191 CE
Ruled by Assyrians, Persians, Alexander the Great, Ptolemaic Egypt, Rome, and Byzantine Empire. Became first Christian-governed Roman province.
Crusaders & Venice
1192 - 1571
Lusignan dynasty established Frankish kingdom. Venice fortified cities against Ottoman expansion.
Ottoman Rule
1571 - 1878
300+ years under Ottoman control. Muslim settlers established Turkish Cypriot community. Greek Cypriots remained 80% majority. Growing desire for union with Greece (enosis).
British Colonial Era
1878 - 1960
British administration from 1878, formal colony from 1925. Greek Cypriots sought enosis; Turkish Cypriots opposed it, fearing persecution. EOKA launched armed campaign in 1955.
Independence & Crisis
1960 - 1963
Independence with complex constitution protecting both communities. Neither side satisfied. Constitutional breakdown led to "Bloody Christmas" violence in 1963.

The Divided Island
1974 - Present
UN Buffer Zone (Green Line) divides island. Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus declared 1983 (recognized only by Turkey). Republic of Cyprus joined EU 2004. Nicosia remains Europe's last divided capital. Multiple reunification attempts have failed, including 2004 Annan Plan.
